# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge
We, the maintainers and contributors of this project, commit to a welcoming, safe, and inclusive community. We value collaboration, curiosity, and mutual respect. We do not tolerate harassment, discrimination, or any behavior that harms participants or the project.

## Scope
This Code applies in all project spaces and when representing the project in public, online or offline. It covers all interactions on issue trackers, pull requests, discussions, chats, social media, events, and any other collaboration channel.

## Expected Behavior
- Be respectful, patient, and constructive.
- Assume good intent. Ask for clarification before judging.
- Use inclusive language. Be mindful of differing backgrounds and experience levels.
- Give and accept feedback gracefully. Focus on ideas, not people.
- Respect boundaries. Honor “no,” “stop,” or equivalent requests.
- Keep discussions on-topic. Take tangents to a separate thread.
- Protect confidentiality and private information.
- Follow project processes and licensing requirements.

## Unacceptable Behavior
- Harassment, intimidation, or stalking (online or in person).
- Hate speech, slurs, or discrimination based on identity or protected characteristics.
- Sexualized language or imagery; unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, personal or political attacks.
- Threats of violence, doxxing, or encouragement of self-harm.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, PRs, or events.
- Unauthorized sharing of others’ private content or contact details.
- Gratuitous or off-topic violent or sexual content.
- Any conduct that violates applicable laws or platform policies.

## Reporting
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:
- Email: alanursapu@gmail.com (maintainer: @AUSP59).
- If reporting a security vulnerability, use the subject: [SECURITY].
- Include: what happened, where/when, people involved, and any supporting evidence. Screenshots and message links help.
- If you are in immediate danger, contact local emergency services first.

We will acknowledge reports within 72 hours and aim to provide an initial response timeline within 7 days. Urgent risks may be addressed immediately.

## Confidentiality and Non-Retaliation
We treat reports as confidential. We share details only with those who need them to resolve the issue. Good-faith reports will not lead to retaliation. Retaliation is itself a violation.

## Enforcement Responsibilities
Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards and taking fair, proportionate action. Maintainers may remove or edit contributions not aligned with this Code, and may issue warnings, temporary restrictions, or permanent bans.

Maintainership requires impartiality. If a conflict of interest exists, the maintainer must recuse and delegate enforcement.

## Enforcement Guidelines
When addressing violations, maintainers will consider the impact and the violator’s history. Possible outcomes include:

1. Correction  
   - Private or public note identifying the issue and required change.  
   - Intended for inadvertent or first-time issues.

2. Warning  
   - Formal notice of violation with expectations for improved behavior.  
   - Further incidents may lead to restrictions.

3. Temporary Restriction  
   - Time-bound limits on participation (e.g., muting, issue/PR bans, event exclusion).  
   - Conditions for return are specified.

4. Permanent Ban  
   - Removal from project spaces and contribution channels.  
   - Reserved for severe, repeated, or malicious misconduct.

## Appeals
If you believe an action was unwarranted or disproportionate, reply to the enforcement notice or email alanursapu@gmail.com with context and any new information. Appeals will be reviewed by maintainers not involved in the original decision when possible.

## Safety Tools and Evidence
Keep records. Save links, timestamps, and screenshots. If you feel unsafe interacting with someone, disengage and report. Maintainers may preserve relevant logs for as long as needed to investigate and comply with legal obligations, then delete them securely.

## Moderation of Content
We may edit, hide, or remove content that violates this Code, is off-topic, reveals private data, or poses security risk. We may lock threads to prevent escalation.

## Contributor Responsibilities
- Follow this Code in all interactions.  
- Report incidents you witness.  
- Respect moderation decisions and use the appeal process when necessary.

## Maintainer Responsibilities
- Apply this Code consistently and transparently.  
- Document decisions and rationale when feasible.  
- Communicate timelines and outcomes to affected parties.

## Updates
This Code may be revised to improve clarity or coverage. Material changes will be noted in the repository history. By participating, you agree to the latest version.
